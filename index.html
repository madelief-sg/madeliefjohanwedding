Getting there

Travel

All roads lead to Vrede en Lust!

1
Step one

Fly into Cape Town (CPT)

Amsterdam has daily direct ~11 hr flights arriving ~21:30 local; alternatively connecting flights via Doha, Dubai, Addis Abbaba or London. Book well in advance and consider when before / after Christmas and New Years you fly — January is peak summer season in South Africa. KLM also frequently has worthwile promotions for Cape Town (Real Deal Days or Airmiles redemption deals), so stay in the loop for those! Alternatively, if you want to do safari while in South Africa, consider flying through Johannesburg on the east of South Africa!

✈️ Cape Town Intl (CPT) 📅 Book early — high season 🕐 ~11 hr from Amsterdam direct flight
2
Step two

Decide your mode of travel

Travel in South Africa is predominantly car-based. Some buses exist but we never use them. Uber is reliable and affordable, especially around Cape Town. If you expect to move around more or go beyond the city, we recommend you hire a car at the airport.

🚕
Uber
Reliable and cheap. Uber works well throughout the Cape Town area, incl. Constantia and the even the winelands with some patience.
~R150–250 airport → city centre
🚗
Hire a car
Great if you're planning a road trip, travelling in a group, or want the freedom to explore South at your own pace. All major rental companies are at the airport. The most budget friendly option in town we know is Rent A Cheapie.
~R300-600 per diem, excl. fuel, extra coverage, extra kilometers.
3
Step three

From Cape Town to the venue

Vrede & Lust is about 55km / 45 min from the airport, or 75 km / 1hr from Cape Town city center. Take the N2 and R45 through the Winelands to Stellenbosch, then Franschhoek. Alternatively, it's 20km / 25 min from Stellenbosch or 12km/ 20 min from Paarl via R45. Vrede en Lust is well signposted. There is plenty of parking at the estate and the nearby other accomodations. From the VNL parking lot, it's 300 m to the reserved part of the estate in the back behind the bistro and tasting room. If you do not have a car, Uber will also take you there for ~R400-500.

Before and/or after the wedding

Where to stay in Cape Town

We can recommend reserving some time before or after the wedding to experience all the Mother City has to offer! You don't need to stay in city center since Uber is readily available, so here are some neighbourhood / area options depending on your vibe.

🌊
Camps Bay & Clifton Beach
Beachside nightlife & villa apartments

The glamorous strip along the Atlantic coast — beautiful beach, restaurants, and bars with mountain views. It's lively and upscale; arguably also the most yuppy, attracting the well-off visitor more than the backpacker.

🏄
Tableview
Watersports valhalla

Where Madelief's dad lives - specifically, near the Dolphin Beach Hotel! A relaxed residential neighbourhood along Table Bay, popular with experienced (kite)surfers for its consistent winds. To get into surfing, go towards Langebaan or Muizenberg. Great if you want a local, less touristy experience.

🌳
Gardens
Leafy & central

A pleasant, safe, a bit quieter neighbourhood on the slopes of Table Mountain. Good mix of guesthouses and apartments; close to plenty bars and restaurant around Kloof Street.

🎨
Woodstock & Observatory
Edgy — nightlife

A creative, up-and-coming area with cool cafés and street art. A lot of students live here, but parts of it can feel rough, especially at night (please take ubers after dark). Ideal if you want to be near the nightlife, but not our top recommendation for guests looking for a more family-friendly holiday in the Cape Town area.

🏠
Greenpoint
Seaside urban

Recently a hotspot for digital nomads. Plenty apartments, and facilites next to the beaches of the Camps and Clifton; under Lion's Head for hikes, and near the stadium and Waterfront for entertainment.

Alternatively, have a look in some areas around the city as you'll be travelling by car anyway. We heard great reviews from Houtbaai (villas for with kids), Muizenberg (beginner surfing) or Constantia (near some excellent wineries and restaurants, more suburban). Prioritise a place you like the look of — you won't be stuck there!

Hikes

Hike

Skeleton Gorge - Table Mountain

Hike up table mountain through the Skeleton Gorge route, which starts at Kirstenbosch. When you reached the top, you have several options for the route down, one of which is walking across the top (1hr) to the cable car.

🚗 30–40 min drive 🧗 3 - 4 hr hike to the top, medium-high intensity
Hike

Platteklip - Table Mountain

Hike straight up Table Mountain on the Cape Town-facing side. Quite intense, but amazing views and the 'quickest' way up (apart from the cable cars).

🚗 20 min drive 🧗 2 - 3 hr hike to the top, high intensity
Hike

Lion's Head

If you only have time for one hike - we recommend this one! Our advice would be to go 7-8 am and grab a nice lunch after or go for an afternoon hike after the heat.

🚗 20 min drive 🧗 2 - 3 hr hike in total (return), high intensity
Hike

Contour path - Table Mountain

Easy scenic hike along Table Mountain with amazing city views. Start in Kirstenbosch for a beautiful forest section before following the contour path.

🧗 2 - 3 hr hike, low intensity 👶 Kid friendly
Hike

Diagonal Cave - Table Mountain

A more adventurous Table Mountain hike featuring steep sections, panoramic views, and the dramatic Diagonal Cave.

🧗 4 - 6 hr hike, moderate-high intensity

Devil's Peak - Table Mountain

Road trips

Adventure

Garden Route

The Garden Route is one of South Africa’s most beautiful road trips, with ocean views, forests, and charming coastal towns along the way. If you have extra time, drive the spectacular Swartberg Pass for dramatic mountain scenery and one of the country’s most unforgettable roads.

🌿️Nature 🚗 4-14 days, depending how much you want to see 📅 Multi-day
Adventure

Cederberg

Cederberg is a mountainous nature reserve, about 3hrs drive north of Cape Town. Great for medium and more challenging hikes, ancient cave paintings and rock formations, a wine estate, and camping / lodges in between all that. If you're coming in from outside, hikes start after 1-1.5 hr drive into the park, so start early! We stayed at Solace Eco Cabin just outside the reserve, highly recommend!

🚗 2-5 days 🌿️Nature ⛺️ Camping 📅 Multi-day
Adventure

Ecocabins & extended winelands

Several wine estates have opportunities for overnight stay. We stayed on an 'glamping' eco cabin in the hillside of Boschendal, called Camp Canoe, with beautiful valley sunset views. Take the opportunity to explore deeper into the winelands, e.g. Tulbagh, Franschhoek, Paarl, Jonkershoek. Alternatively, only a short taxi drive away from the wine tram station!

🚗 2-3 days 🌿️Nature ⛺️Glamping 📅 Multi-day
